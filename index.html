This animated proof follows the same methodology outlined in Euclid's Elements: Proposition 47

Claim: for a given side a or b, the area of the moving triangle does not change.
Observe: we have two kinds of motion; rotation and skewing. Clearly the area of the rotating triangle isn't changing. Also,

area(triangle) = 1/2 base x height

even for obtuse triangles. So if triangle abc is right, the height of the skewing triangle doesn't change, nor does its area. QED
Therefore, it's plain to see from watching the animation: if triangle abc is right then a2 + b2 = c2
