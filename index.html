Euclid's Elements: Proposition 47

Claim: the area of the triangle in motion never changes as it embarks from the square above and continues down to the corresponding rectangle below.

Notice that we have two kinds of motion: rotation and skewing. Clearly the area of the rotating triangle isn't changing so let's turn our attention to the skewing process: Refresh the page and follow the motion of the green triangle as its tip traces along side b. Now consider the fact that:

area of a triangle = ½ (base × height)

even for obtuse triangles. Triangle abc is right(the angle between sides a and b is 90°) so side b is perpendicular to side a and parallel to the base of the skewing triangle. So the height of the skewing triangle never changes, neither does its area. The area of the skewing triangle following the rotation doesn't change either since its height remains the same throughout the skewing process. QED baby

The argument above also holds true for the blue triangle starting from side b. In both cases the animation shows that:

½ area(square) = ½ area(rectangle)

Hence:

area(square) = area(rectangle)

Combining the results for both sides a and b gives us:

a2 + b2 = c2
