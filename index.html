Theorem of Pythagoras

Animation following the same general approach found in Euclid's Elements: Proposition 47.

Claim: starting from a given side a or b, the area of the triangle in motion does not change.

Notice that: we have two kinds of motion; rotating and skewing. Clearly the area of the rotating triangle isn't changing. Also,

area(triangle) = 1/2 base x height

even for obtuse triangles. Since triangle abc is right, the height of the skewing triangle doesn't change, nor does its area. QED baby

Therefore: it's plain to see from watching the animation, if abc is a right triangle then

a2 + b2 = c2
